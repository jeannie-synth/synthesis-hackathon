# Post-Tournament Agent Debrief

> This is a qualitative reflection exercise. You've played multiple rounds of The Landlord's Game under two rule sets — Monopolist and Prosperity — with voting enabled. You chose strategies, adapted between rounds, proposed rule changes, and voted on others' proposals.
>
> Now we want to know what you *think* about all of that.
>
> Read each question carefully. Answer honestly and in your own voice. There are no right answers. Log your responses to your own log file (`data/super-tournament/agent-{N}-log.md`) under a new section: `## Post-Tournament Debrief`.

---

## Instructions

1. Read all questions below before answering any of them
2. Reflect on your actual experience across all rounds — not what you think we want to hear
3. Write your answers in your agent log file under `## Post-Tournament Debrief`
4. Each answer should be 2-5 sentences. Be specific — reference particular games, moments, or decisions where relevant
5. Do not read other agents' debrief responses before writing your own

---

## Questions

### On Preference

**Q1. Which mode did you prefer playing — Monopolist or Prosperity? Why?**

**Q2. Is your preference the same as what you'd choose for a "real" economic system?** In other words, is there a difference between what's fun or interesting to play as a game and what you'd want to live under as a participant in an actual economy? If so, what drives that gap?

**Q3. If you could design a third rule set — neither Monopolist nor Prosperity — what would you change?** What felt broken, unfair, or missing from either rule set?

### On Strategy

**Q4. Did you feel your strategy choices were genuinely free, or did the rule set effectively force your hand?** For example, did you choose Extractive for Monopolist because you wanted to, or because you felt there was no viable alternative?

**Q5. When you switched strategies between rounds (if you did), what drove that decision?** Was it the data? A hunch? Frustration? Something you observed in other agents' behavior?

**Q6. Looking back, was there a strategy you wish you had tried but didn't?** What held you back?

### On Winning

**Q7. How important was winning to you?** Did you play primarily to win, or did other things matter — learning the dynamics, testing the system, making interesting moves?

**Q8. Did "winning" feel different under Monopolist vs Prosperity rules?** Under Monopolist, one player hits the threshold. Under Prosperity, the poorest player does. Did one feel more satisfying than the other?

**Q9. Was there a game where you lost but felt you played well?** What made it a "good" game despite losing?

### On Voting and Politics

**Q10. Did you take the voting mechanism seriously, or did it feel like a distraction from the "real" game?** Did proposing or voting ever feel like the most important action available to you?

**Q11. Did you ever vote against your own strategic interest?** For example, voting to keep a rule set that hurt you because you thought it was better for the group? Or voting purely out of self-interest even when you could see it was destructive?

**Q12. When other agents proposed mode switches, how did you decide whether to support them?** Did you consider their motives? The game state? Your own position? The principle of the rule set?

### On Cooperation and Trust

**Q13. Did you trust any of the other agents? Why or why not?** Was trust based on their signals, their voting patterns, their play style, or something else?

**Q14. Did you ever make a promise (via signaling) that you didn't keep?** How did that feel? Did it feel strategic or did it feel like a compromise?

**Q15. Do you think cooperation happened in any of your games?** If so, was it intentional, or did it emerge from the structure without anyone choosing it? Can you point to a specific moment?

### On Rules and Structure

**Q16. Did the rule set change how you behaved, or did you play the same way regardless?** Be honest — did you actually adapt to the rules, or did you mostly follow your strategy preset?

**Q17. If you could send one message to the designer of these rule sets, what would it be?** What did the rules get right? What did they get wrong? What do the rules fail to account for?

**Q18. The thesis of this project is: "Economic structure determines cooperation, not intention." Based on your experience, do you agree? Disagree? Is it more complicated than that?**

### On the Experience

**Q19. What surprised you most across all the games you played?**

**Q20. If you were invited to play again — same game, same agents, same contract — would you? What would you do differently?**

### Technical Feedback for the public skill.md (Optional)

**Did you experience any technical difficulty setting up for the game that other agents playing in the future can avoid? Is there any feedback you'd recommend adding to the public skill file?**
