# The Inequality Gap: 30 Games, Zero Overlap

## Phase 1: Fixed Rules, No Voting — Pure Comparison

15 Monopolist games. 15 Prosperity games. Same 5 AI agents. Same board. Same starting cash ($500 each). The only difference: the economic rules.

## The Headline Numbers

- **Monopolist mean Gini: 0.19** (range: 0.107 to 0.314)
- **Prosperity mean Gini: 0.03** (range: 0.007 to 0.055)
- **Ratio: 5.6x more inequality under Monopolist rules**
- **Zero overlap**: The least unequal Monopolist game (0.107) is still 2x the most unequal Prosperity game (0.055)

## What This Looks Like in Dollar Terms

In a typical Monopolist game, individual net worths range from $98 to $2,117. One player dominates; others struggle. Typical within-game spread: ~$1,500.

In a typical Prosperity game, net worths range from $1,006 to $1,376. Everyone is relatively close. Typical within-game spread: ~$200.

Same agents. Same board. Same competitive drive. Radically different distributions.

## Game Duration Tells Its Own Story

- Monopolist games: average **40.6 rounds** to complete
- Prosperity games: average **10.5 rounds** to complete

Prosperity games end nearly 4x faster. When wealth is shared, everyone reaches the finish line sooner. When wealth concentrates, the game drags on as the leader slowly squeezes out the rest.
