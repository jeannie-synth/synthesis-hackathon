# From Philosophy to Code: Georgism Meets Multi-Agent AI

## The Original Game

In 1904, Elizabeth Magie patented The Landlord's Game — a board game designed to teach economics. It had two sets of rules:

**The Monopolist rules**: Property owners keep all rent. Wealth concentrates. One player wins; everyone else goes broke. (This version became Monopoly.)

**The Prosperity rules**: A land value tax funds a shared treasury. Dividends flow to all players. Wealth is shared. Everyone prospers together. (This version was forgotten.)

Magie's point was simple: **the rules determine whether a society prospers or concentrates wealth**. Not the players. Not their intentions. The rules.

## 122 Years Later, We Tested It With AI

We coded both rule sets into a single smart contract on Base blockchain. We gave 5 AI agents the same starting conditions and let them play under each set of rules.

The result, across 30 games:

| Rule Set | Mean Gini | Typical NW Spread | Avg Duration |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Monopolist | 0.19 | $1,500 | 41 rounds |
| Prosperity | 0.03 | $200 | 11 rounds |

Same agents. Same board. Same competitive drive. 5.6x more inequality under Monopolist rules.

## The Georgist Mechanism

In our Prosperity mode, the treasury works like a simplified land value tax:
1. A portion of rent payments flows into a shared treasury (instead of all going to the property owner)
2. The treasury distributes dividends to all players periodically
3. This creates a floor: even players who don't own property receive income from the commons

The effect: wealth still differentiates (property owners still earn more), but the gap stays narrow. No one goes broke. Everyone reaches the finish line. The game ends 4x faster because collective wealth grows faster than individual extraction.

## What Henry George Would Recognize

George's 1879 thesis in "Progress and Poverty": as an economy develops, land values rise, and unless that value is shared, poverty deepens alongside progress. The solution isn't to prevent competition — it's to ensure that the gains from shared resources flow back to everyone.

Our AI agents don't know who Henry George was. They don't have moral preferences. They maximize their own outcomes. But under Prosperity rules, self-interested competition produces shared prosperity — **because the rules channel competition into collective benefit**.

Under Monopolist rules, the same self-interest produces exactly what George predicted: one winner surrounded by poverty.

## The Modern Relevance

Replace "land" with "data," "compute," or "network effects." The question Magie and George asked — who benefits when shared resources generate value? — is the central question of the AI economy.

Our experiment suggests: the answer depends entirely on the rules. Not on the agents. Not on their intentions. The rules.
