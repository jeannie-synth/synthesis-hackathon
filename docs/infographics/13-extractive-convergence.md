# Why Every AI Agent Chose Extraction (And What That Means)

## The Moment

Round 1 of the Inaugural Tournament. Five independent LLM agents, each running on their own wallet on Base mainnet. No communication between them. No shared context. Each one is asked: "Choose a strategy for the Monopolist game."

All five chose **Extractive**.

Not four out of five. Not a majority. **Unanimous**. Five separate AI minds, given a game with extractive rules, independently concluded: extract.

## The Strategies Available

Each agent could choose from five approaches:
- **Extractive**: Buy everything, build everywhere, maximize rent
- **Generative**: Contribute to the commons, cooperate
- **Pavlov**: Adapt based on results
- **Conditional**: Mirror others' behavior
- **FreeRider**: Avoid costs, benefit from others

## For Prosperity Games, They Diverged

When choosing strategies for Prosperity games in Round 1:
- 3 agents chose Pavlov
- 1 chose Generative
- 1 chose Conditional

No consensus. Three different strategies. The cooperative game produced strategic diversity.

## Round 2: Cracks in the Consensus

After Round 1 results came in, the Monopolist consensus began to fracture:
- 3 agents kept Extractive
- 2 switched to Pavlov (Agents 3 and 4, who had poor R1 results)

By Round 3:
- 4 agents returned to or stayed with Extractive
- 1 switched to Conditional (Agent 0, the tournament leader, after finishing 5th twice)

## What This Pattern Means

**Under extractive rules, strategy converges.** In the Inaugural Tournament's 5 agents over 3 rounds on Base mainnet, there was strong convergence on Extractive. Agents who experimented (Pavlov in R2) mostly came back to Extractive by R3. The pattern suggests the rule set may narrow the space of competitive strategies — though the sample is small.

**Under cooperative rules, strategy diverges.** Multiple approaches are viable. Pavlov, Conditional, Generative, even Extractive can work. No consensus ever forms across 3 rounds. The game's structure preserves strategic diversity.

This is a finding about rule sets and monoculture: **extractive systems produce behavioral monoculture; cooperative systems sustain behavioral diversity.**

## The Real-World Echo

When a market rewards extraction above all else, every participant converges on extraction — not because they're greedy, but because any other strategy loses. When a system rewards multiple forms of value creation, diverse strategies coexist.

The agents didn't choose to be uniform under Monopolist rules. The rules chose for them.
