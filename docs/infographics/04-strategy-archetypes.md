# Five Strategies, Two Worlds: Who Thrives Where?

## The Cast

Five rule-based strategies competed across 13 Phase 2 games with voting enabled. Each embodies a different economic philosophy:

- **Extractive**: Buy everything, build everywhere, maximize rent extraction. The capitalist maximizer.
- **Generative**: Contribute to the commons, cooperate when possible. The idealist.
- **Pavlov**: Win-stay, lose-switch. If winning, keep doing what you're doing. If losing, change. The pragmatist.
- **Conditional**: Mirror what others do. Cooperate if others cooperate, defect if others defect. The reciprocator.
- **FreeRider**: Never buy property, avoid costs, live off the system. The parasite.

## The Key Insight: Mode Gap

The **Mode Gap** column reveals which strategies are most sensitive to the rule set:

**Extractive has the widest gap ($163)**. It earns $1,329 under Monopolist rules (3rd place) but drops to $1,166 under Prosperity rules (last place). Extraction pays when the rules allow it and punishes when they don't. This is the strategy most shaped by structure.

**Generative has almost no gap ($4)**. It earns nearly the same regardless of the rule set. The idealist neither thrives nor suffers from the rules — it performs moderately everywhere.

**Pavlov leads both overall and in Monopolist games** ($1,481 M-start, highest of any strategy). Its adaptive "win-stay, lose-switch" approach works best when the environment is turbulent — and Monopolist games with voting are turbulent.

**Conditional ties Pavlov in total wins (4 each) but dominates Prosperity** (3 of 6 Prosperity-start wins). When cooperation is the norm, mirroring the group is the winning move.

## The Philosophical Read

The rule set doesn't just change outcomes — it changes which behaviors succeed. Under Monopolist rules, the pragmatist (Pavlov) thrives by adapting fast. Under Prosperity rules, the reciprocator (Conditional) thrives by reflecting the cooperative norm. The extractor succeeds only when extraction is rewarded by the structure.

**Character doesn't change. Context does. And context determines who wins.**
